Real versus nominal returns. A 7 percent gain in a 3 percent inflation year is really 4 percent. Nominal return is the number on the statement. Real return subtracts inflation — the loss of purchasing power. Money in a “safe” account earning 2 percent while prices rise 3 percent is quietly shrinking in real terms. Inflation is the tax nobody sends you a bill for. The takeaway. Always ask “return after inflation?” — real return is what actually grows your buying power. Here is a question. If an investment returns 6 percent while inflation is 3 percent, the real return is about… A. 9 percent. B. 6 percent. C. 3 percent. D. 0 percent.
